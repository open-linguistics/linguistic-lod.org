The UKP Lab is pleased to announce the publication of <http://www.morganclaypoolpublishers.com/catalog_Orig/product_info.php?cPath=22&series=29&products_id=958>


Linked Lexical Knowledge Bases: Foundations and Applications<http://www.morganclaypoolpublishers.com/catalog_Orig/product_info.php?cPath=22&series=29&products_id=958>,


a book by Iryna Gurevych<https://www.ukp.tu-darmstadt.de/people/group-heads/prof-dr-iryna-gurevych/>, Judith Eckle-Kohler<https://www.ukp.tu-darmstadt.de/people/senior-staff/dr-judith-eckle-kohler/>, and Michael Matuschek<https://www.linkedin.com/in/michael-matuschek-09449566>.


The book conveys the fundamentals of linked lexical knowledge bases and sheds light on their different aspects from various perspectives, focusing on their construction and use in natural language processing. It characterizes a wide range of both expert-based and collaboratively constructed lexical knowledge bases.


Print and digital editions are available for purchase from bookstores and from the publisher, Morgan & Claypool<http://www.morganclaypoolpublishers.com/catalog_Orig/product_info.php?cPath=22&series=29&products_id=958>.
